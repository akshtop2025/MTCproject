About the course

This rigorous training is tailored for practicing Obstetricians and Gynaecologists seeking to enhance their expertise in ultrasound imaging for women's health. The course offers a perfect blend of theoretical knowledge and hands-on experience, covering the full spectrum of ultrasound applications in Obstetrics, Gynecology, and Infertility. Participants will observe live demonstrations of ultrasound assessments and have the opportunity to perform scans under expert guidance.

A key feature of this program is its focus on practical skills. Trainees will gain extensive hands-on experience using state-of-the-art equipment, including the advanced GE Voluson E6 ultrasound machine. The course places special emphasis on mastering the Nuchal Translucency (NT) Scan, a critical skill in modern obstetric care.

Through lectures, video presentations, and interactive sessions, participants will deepen their understanding of ultrasound principles and interpretation. The curriculum covers a wide range of scenarios, ensuring young Gynecologists are prepared to handle diverse clinical situations.

By the end of this course, participants will have acquired the knowledge, skills, and confidence to incorporate advanced ultrasound techniques into their practice, enhancing patient care and diagnostics in Obstetrics, Gynecology, and Infertility Management.

Course Highlights:

Live Demonstrations:

  • Observation of ultrasound assessments in Obstetrics, Gynecological conditions, Infertility cases

  • Opportunity for participants to perform these assessments under supervision

Theoretical Education:

  • Lectures on ultrasound applications in
    a) Obstetrics b) Gynecology c) Infertility

  • Video presentations demonstrating various ultrasound techniques and findings

Hands-on Experience:

  • Practical sessions for performing ultrasounds in
    a) Obstetric cases b) Gynecological conditions

Specialized Scanning Techniques:

  • Training in performing Nuchal Translucency (NT) Scan

  • Use of advanced equipment: GE Voluson E6 ultrasound machine

Equipment Familiarization:

  • Introduction to and practice with the GE Voluson E6 ultrasound machine

  • Understanding machine settings and optimization for different types of scans

Case-based Learning:

  • Includes a variety of cases to cover different scenarios in Obstetrics, Gynecology, and Infertility

Image Interpretation:

  • Training in reading and interpreting ultrasound images across various conditions

Practical Assessment:

  • Includes an evaluation of participants' scanning skills and image interpretation abilities

What will you learn:

FELLOWSHIP (Hands-on): Basic Sonography

  • Physics of Ultrasonography & Machine Settings

  • Basics of Early Pregnancy Ultrasound & Dating at 8-10 weeks scan

  • Pregnancy of uncertain viability & unknown location

  • Fetal biometry & growth charts

  • Cervical Assessment for Preterm Labour

  • Basics of Gynec Ultrasound: Uterus, Ovaries, Adenaxa

  • How to do reporting after a scan

FELLOWSHIP (Hands-on): Advanced Sonography

  • Opportunity to learn, observe, perform & discuss the process and protocols
    with the expert trainers and clear your doubts with them.

  • Physics of Ultrasonography & Machine Settings

  • Patient counseling before ultrasonography

  • Learn to perform 3D Evolution of uterus, baby’s face, spine,
    heart (through stic mode) & brain (through TUI mode)

  • Understand the use of 3D/4D in Fetal Echo, Fetal doppler,
    intra uterine fetal blood transfusion

  • Dating Scan & evaluation of multiple pregnancy

  • Pregnancy of uncertain viability, USG in Tubal Ectopic & Scar Ectopic

  • Fetal Biometry & Growth Charts

  • Cervical Assessment for Preterm Labour & induction of labour

  • Role of Doppler in Obstetrics

  • First-trimester Screening for Down Syndrome & NT Scan

  • First Trimester Anomaly Detection

  • Screening for Preeclampsia

  • TIFFA Scan (Anomaly scan)

  • Basics of Gynec Ultrasound: Uterus, Ovaries, Adnexa

  • Ultrasound in Infertility- Follicular monitoring

  • How to do reporting after a scan
